K.I.D.S

K.I.D.S (2010) - Mixtape Released in 2010, "K.I.D.S" (Kickin' Incredibly Dope Sh*t) was one of Mac Miller's early mixtapes. It helped him gain significant attention and a growing fan base. Included tracks like "Kool Aid & Frozen Pizza" and "Nikes on My Feet." Marked his early exploration of hip-hop.

Best Day Ever

Blue Slide Park (2011) Debut studio album. Named after a park in Pittsburgh. Features tracks like "Frick Park Market" and "Donald Trump." Achieved commercial success and established Mac Miller as a rising star.

GOOD A.M

Third studio album. Tackled themes of addiction and personal growth. Contains singles like "100 Grandkids" and "Weekend." Considered a critical and commercial success.

Divine Feminine

Fourth studio album. Explores themes of love and relationships. Features collaborations with artists like Ariana Grande and Kendrick Lamar. Known for tracks such as "Dang!" and "Cinderella."

Faces

Faces (2014) - Mixtape "Faces" is a mixtape that was released in 2014. Known for its dark and introspective themes. Features tracks like "Diablo" and "Wedding." Showcased Mac Miller's artistic growth and lyrical depth.

Swimming

Fifth studio album. Released shortly before Mac Miller's passing. Reflects a more mature and contemplative sound. Includes songs like "Self Care" and "Ladders."

Circles

Circles (2020) - Posthumous Album Released posthumously in 2020 as a companion album to "Swimming." Features a more introspective and contemplative style. Includes tracks like "Good News" and "Complicated." Served as a poignant and fitting tribute to Mac Miller's artistic journey. Mac Miller's mixtapes and albums, including "Circles," showcase his evolution as an artist and his enduring impact on the hip-hop and music industry.
